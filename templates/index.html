about

Nebula is a publication exploring intricacies of the collective, cooperative, anarchist, utopian and open source organizing, and strategies for non-hierarchical models and consensus decision-making. Showcasing projects and people creating collaborative futures.

Already in the early 1990s there was a growing number of alternative organizational forms appearing, whether inspired by anarchism, syndicalism, the ecological movement, the co-operative movement, libertarian communism, self-help groups or by feminism. These organizations offered an alternative to the dominant form of the capitalist business enterprise, repressing autonomous human development. Nevertheless, most studies of management have focused attention to power relations in mainstream capitalist organizations. This has sidelined the development of alternative, more humane, ways of organizing that arise globally, which we want to explore in our publication.

The aim of this publication is to create a bridge between the existing and operating cooperative, collective, anarchist, utopian, and social impact projects having real impact with the decentralized governance world as envisioned by the DAO community. We want to explore the intricacies of those groups and find out how they organize, experiment with non-hierarchical models and consensus decision-making, what are their principles and strategies, how do they approach the question of leadership, what kind of new forms of organizational practices and subjectivities do they cultivate.
